Jimi Hendrix

Voodoo Child

Here's a time line of Jimi's life:

  • 1942 - Jimi is born in Seattle, WA.
  • 1958 - Jimi buys his first guitar for $5.
  • 1959 - Jimi's father buys him his first electric guitar, a white Supro Ozark.
  • 1959 - Jimi joins the Rocking Kings.
  • 1961 - Jimi joins the Army.
  • 1962 - Jimi receives an honorable discharge from the Army, on the basis of unsuitability, due (in part) to more interest in playing his guitar.
  • 1963 - Jimi moves to Tennessee, spending time playing with his band, the King Kasuals, and performing as a backing musician for muscians such as Wilson Pickett, Slim Harpo, Sam Cooke, Ike & Tina Turner, and Jackie Wilson.
  • 1964 - Jimi moves to Harlem and becomes the guitarist with the Isley Brothers back-up band, the I.B. Specials.
  • 1965 - Jimi joins Little Richard's touring band, the Upsetters.
  • 1966 - Jimi is recruited by Chas Chandler , who brings him to London and recuits the members of the Jimi Hendrix Experience.
  • 1966 - The Jimi Hendrix Experience plays their very first show at the Novelty in Evreux.
  • 1966 - The band plays a 15-minute performance at the Olympia theatre in Paris, which marks the earliest known recoding.
  • 1967 - The Jimi Hendrix Experience releases their first full-length, LP Are You Experienced?
  • 1967 - The Jimi Hendrix Experience playes the Monterey Pop Festival, where, after an iconic set, Jimi destroyed his guitar and tossed pieces of it out to the audience. The performance was filmed, and later included in the concert documentary Monterey Pop, which helped Hendrix gain popularity with the U.S. public.
  • 1967 - The Jimi Hendrix experience releases Axis: Bold as Love, recorded to fulfill the Experience's contract, which stated that they had to produce two records in 1967.
  • 1968 - Axis: Bold as Love reaches number three in the U.S.
  • 1968 - The Jimi Hendrix experience releases Electric Ladyland, the third and final studio album, whichi goes on to hit number one in the U.S.
  • 1969 - The Jimi Hendrix experience disbands, due to conflicts between Noel Redding and Jimi.
  • 1969 - Jimi plays the Woodstock Music and Art Fair, and his performance is later immortalized in the 1970 documentary film,Woodstock
  • 1970 - Jimi dies in London, UK.
